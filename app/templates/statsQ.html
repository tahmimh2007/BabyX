AP Statistics Practice

1. Which of the following is a measure of central tendency?

A) Range
B) Variance
C) Median
D) Standard Deviation

2. In a normal distribution, approximately what percent of data falls within one standard deviation of the mean?

A) 34%
B) 68%
C) 95%
D) 99.7%

3. What is the purpose of random assignment in an experiment?

A) Ensure every subject gets every treatment
B) Reduce bias and create comparable groups
C) Select the sample from the population
D) Maximize variability

4. If two events A and B are independent, what is P(A and B)?

A) P(A) + P(B)
B) P(A) × P(B)
C) P(A) / P(B)
D) P(A) − P(B)

5. Which graphical display is most appropriate for showing the distribution of a single quantitative variable?

A) Bar graph
B) Boxplot
C) Pie chart
D) Two-way table

6. Which of the following conditions must be checked before performing a one-sample t-test?

A) Randomness, Normality, Independence
B) Randomness, Equal proportions, Normality
C) Equal means, Randomness, Normality
D) Independence, Proportionality, Randomness

7. The correlation coefficient r measures the strength and direction of what type of relationship?

A) Categorical
B) Nonlinear
C) Linear
D) Exponential

8. In hypothesis testing, what is a Type I error?

A) Failing to reject a false null hypothesis
B) Rejecting a true null hypothesis
C) Rejecting a false null hypothesis
D) Accepting a true alternative hypothesis

9. A researcher wants to estimate the proportion of students who prefer online classes. Which sampling method ensures every student has an equal chance of selection?

A) Convenience sampling
B) Voluntary response sampling
C) Simple random sampling
D) Stratified sampling

10. Which of the following statements about p-values is correct?

A) A large p-value indicates strong evidence against the null hypothesis
B) A small p-value indicates weak evidence against the null hypothesis
C) A small p-value indicates strong evidence against the null hypothesis
D) A p-value is never used to make decisions in hypothesis testing

Answer Key

  • 1: C
  • 2: B
  • 3: B
  • 4: B
  • 5: B
  • 6: A
  • 7: C
  • 8: B
  • 9: C
  • 10: C
